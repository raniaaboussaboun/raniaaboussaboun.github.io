About Me

I am a Quantitative Trading Analyst working at the intersection of financial markets, algorithmic trading, and systematic strategy development, with live exposure to European power markets (Intraday, Day-Ahead, and Imbalance trading).

My work combines market microstructure analysis, macroeconomic research, statistical modeling, and machine learning to develop and refine trading signals across FX, equities, commodities, and energy markets such as electricity, gold, and oil.

I focus on building and evaluating systematic strategies using probability theory, stochastic processes, time-series analysis, Monte Carlo simulation, Markov chains, volatility models (GARCH/ARCH), and modern machine learning techniques including neural networks, gradient boosting (XGBoost/LightGBM), and reinforcement learning.

I am particularly interested in how liquidity, order flow, and volatility regimes interact with news and macroeconomic data, and how these inefficiencies can be transformed into robust, risk-adjusted trading strategies.

Outside of my 9–5 role, I actively study markets, develop trading models, and continuously analyze price behavior to improve my understanding of systematic trading and market dynamics.

I also value discipline and performance outside of finance — I regularly go to the gym and treat training as part of maintaining focus, consistency, and long-term execution mindset.
